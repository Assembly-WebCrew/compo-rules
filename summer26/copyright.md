## On Copyright

Every year a few entries run into copyright trouble, almost always by accident.
This section explains where the line is. If your case is not covered, ask before
the deadline at compo-contact@assembly.org or on the #demoscene channel of the
Assembly Discord. Asking is free.

**Why we are strict.** Assembly pays real prize money, entries are recorded and
stay online for years, and Finnish law has no "fair use". A detail that passes
unnoticed during the compo is still searchable a decade later. The burden of
proof is on you: if we have to ask, you need to show where the material came
from and what allows you to use it. An entry we cannot clear is not shown.

**The basics.** Copyright applies automatically. No copyright notice does not
mean free, being publicly available does not mean free, and there is no minimum
length below which copying is safe.

**Other people's material.** Samples of released recordings, covers, remixes and
rearrangements all need permission from the rights holder, and we do not allow
them. A permission from the author personally is not enough if they belong to a
collecting society such as Teosto or Gramex, because they no longer administer
those rights themselves. Sample libraries sold to be used in music are fine;
recordings sold to be listened to are not.

**Creative Commons, public domain and stock.** Allowed where the competition's
rules permit, on two conditions: follow the licence, and declare it in the entry
form with a link to the source. Undeclared licensed material is treated as
unlicensed. Note that NonCommercial licences are not safe here, because Assembly
is a commercial event that pays prize money.

Using licensed third-party material does not break the "previously unreleased"
rule. That rule is about your own work: the entry and its main creative effort
must not have been released before. A licensed font or instrument library does
not make your entry "released" as long as it is not the main effort. If you are
unsure which side of that line you are on, ask before the deadline.

**Trademarks and logos.** Not allowed, not even as a retro homage, and this
includes redrawn versions. Trademarks are protected separately from copyright,
and entries are archived and re-shown afterwards. Evoke the era, the hardware
and the palette instead: none of that belongs to anyone.

**Fan art.** Not allowed, however well made. A practical test: if an average
viewer would name the source when looking at your entry, it is fan art; if they
would only name a style or a mood, it is inspiration. A painting in the style of
a known illustrator with your own subject is fine. A painting of a named
character from a game or a film is not. Borderline cases: send us a description
or a work-in-progress shot and we will answer in writing. Do not rely on parody,
Finnish law gives it far less room than some other countries.

**If something is wrong.** The entry is disqualified and not shown. If the
problem surfaces afterwards, the result can be revised and prize money withheld.
Document your sources in the entry form and in a readme: what the material is,
where it came from, and under what licence. Entries with clean documentation
almost never become a problem.

Finnish rights organisations: Teosto and Gramex (music), Kopiosto
(publications), Kuvasto (visual arts), IFPI Finland (recordings).
